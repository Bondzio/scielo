This article seeks to analyze the real state rental market in Salvador, Brazil. Initially, we describe the housing problem in Salvador based on the guidelines and actions foreseen in the Municipal Plan for Social Interest Housing (PMHIS). Then we treat the policies implemented by state and federal administrations, emphasizing the experience of Social Leasehold Housing (PAR), and including a few considerations on My Home, My Life program. We also approach the issue of the rental market in Salvador based on the profile of dwellers and on the dynamics of the market.
