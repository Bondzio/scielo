This article presents an overview of &#8220;religion and media&#8221;, an interdisciplinary field                of research inquiry that brings together scholarship in anthropology, communication                studies, art history, and religious studies, among others. The article provides an                account of the current state of the field and it also outlines some of the key                topics, questions, and debates that have defined research in this area. The article                concludes by proposing some questions and proposals for new research directions in                religion and media.
