Discussing a feminist theoretical project from Latin America requires exposing a series of debates which involves considerations from both post-colonial and post-structuralist theories. Thus, this article aims at focusing on the theory generated from the "third world", from a country (or a set of countries) of the global South, which would open a potential space for dialogue within the production in the mainstream of political knowledge, as well as with the several perspectives ded in what we can call Western "feminist political theory".
